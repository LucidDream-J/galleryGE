Cat 24: Kamisaka SEKKA (1866- 1942)

Sekka was an important artistic figure in early twentieth-century Japan. In 1901, he was sent by the Japanese government to Glasgow where he was heavily influenced by Art Nouveau. Returning to Japan, he taught at the newly opened Kyoto Municipal School of Arts and Crafts, experimented with Western tastes, styles, and methods, and incorporated them into his otherwise traditional Rinpa-style works which accentuate the flatness of the image surface and emphasis pattern and design. Eight prints from the series World of Things (Momoyo-gusa) first published in three accordion style albums by Unsodo, 1909 Unsodo subsequently re-issued individual prints using the original carved blocks.

Birds and Reeds in Winter

Cat 28: Torri KOTONDO (1900 – 1976)

One of the great artists of the first half of the 20th Century, Kotondo excelled in the art of the beautiful women print. However, his output was very small (only 22) and the number of each image also small. As with Goyo, with the permission of his heirs, small runs of a limited edition of his beauty prints were produced, in this case by the publisher Ishukankokai.

Cat 30: Tsuchiya KOITSU (1870 – 1949)

Like Hatsui, Koitsu Tsuchiya's prints celebrate Japan's scenic beauty. Through subtle effects of light and shadow, Koitsu endowed his work with an aura of bewitching beauty. .Although he studied with ukiyo-e masters from the age of 15 and worked as a lithographer in the 1920s, he is known for his Japanese landscape prints in the Shin Hanga style which he perfected under the guidance of the publisher, Watanabe, from his meeting with him in 1931.

Mt. Fuji Seen from Lake Sai

Cat 31: Ohara KOSON 1877-1945

Ohara Koson is considered by many to be the foremost 20th century designer of bird and flower prints, (kacho-e). His designs for a primarily Western market and range from haunting realism to humorous depictions of animals at play. His prints continue to be appreciated as among the finest examples of animal and botanical portraits produced by the Shin-hanga (New Prints) movement.

Cat 35: Tsuchiya RAKUSAN (1896 - 1976)

Rakusan Tsuchiya was born in Kyoto and studied art under the great Kyoto artist, Seiho Takeuchi. Rakusan spent the two years between mid 1933 and mid 1935 producing two parallel series of woodblock prints: Koushisei Print Selection and 100 Birds and Flowers. His detailed and intricately coloured woodblock prints were sold by his studio before W.W.II until around 1948.
